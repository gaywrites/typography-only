Pokémon is a multimedia franchise that began as a video game series in 1996, created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori.
At its core, Pokémon (short for "Pocket Monsters") revolves around fictional creatures that humans, known as Pokémon Trainers, catch and train to battle each other for sport.
Players take on the role of Pokémon Trainers with the goal of catching and training Pokémon to become stronger and compete in battles.
There are various species of Pokémon, each with unique abilities, types, and attributes, and they can evolve into more powerful forms and learn new moves as they gain experience.
Battles are turn-based, where Trainers use their Pokémon's moves strategically to defeat opponents, earning badges, experience points, and other rewards.
In the games, Trainers often travel through regions to defeat Gym Leaders and collect badges, with the ultimate goal of becoming the Champion of the Pokémon League.

Pokémon has become a global phenomenon, influencing pop culture and spawning a competitive gaming community.
It is enjoyed by people of all ages, whether they're playing the games, watching the TV series, or trading cards.
The franchise offers a rich and engaging world that combines adventure, strategy, and a sense of camaraderie.
